Rivers and war

Columbia River hydroelectric dams powered WWII-era shipbuilders and smelters that made aluminum for Boeing's B-17. Nationally, dams generated enough power to make nearly 70,000 airplanes and 5,000 ships and tanks during the war. Hydropower rose in the post-war industrial boom.
